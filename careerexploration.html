Career in X-Rays

 

 

 

 

Career Information

Operating x-rays machines require a professional because it is a very precise and sensitive machine. These careers fall under different types of radiologist for radiography. They may vary about the purpose of their profession from techincian, engineer, therapist to a specialist. Being in one of these careers means that you are usually in a hospital or clinical settings as the machines needed are mostly stationary. Hazards that are related to this is radiation and chemical exposure, which means they are at risk of cancer or other lethal conditions. Safe workplace rules must be practiced to reduce accidents. These jobs usually stay in a hospital as their specialist, but they may also get called to other hospitals to help with diagnosis or research.

Radiologic Technologist Runs X-ray machines and other diagnostic imaging procedures to assist doctors in making an appropriate diagnosis and treatment plan for diseases
Radiation Therapist Someone who specializes in treating patients with radiation
Radiologist Specializes in detecting and treating ailments by utilizing imaging technologies.

 

 

Responsibilities and Education

Education requirements vary from each country but in this exploration I am focusing in Saskatchewan, Canada. All of the options above need at least two to three years in their respective fields. I've also chosen these careers to be in clinical and medicine than laboratory.

 

Radiologic Technologist and Radiation Therapist

  • Completion of a two to three year college or other approved program in diagnostic radiography or magnetic resonance imaging and a period of supervised practical training are required.
  • Licensure with a regulatory body (Saskatchewan Association of Medical Radiation Technologists) and certification by the Canadian Association of Medical Radiation Technologists are required

 

Radiologist

  • A bachelor's degree or, completion of a college program and one year of pre-medicine university studies are usually required.
  • A medical school degree that has been authorized as well as specialized training are necessary.
  • Four to five years of specialty residency training are required.
  • Completion of the certifying examinations of the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada

 

 

Job Salary

These are adjusted by the median salary of the professions across the country and are accounted to inflation as of 2024.

Radiologic Technologist $140,000 / year
Radiation Therapist $120,000 / year
Radiologist $250,000 / year

 

 

Positive Features

  1. Great salary, being a radiologist pays a lot.
  2. High demand, These three careers have plenty of job opportunities and a positive outlook.
  3. Techonology used in radiology is constantly evolving. It is also an opportunity for research and innovation.

Negative Features

  1. Long-term education and training. Becoming a radiologist requires a significant commitment to education.
  2. The position can be physically demanding. It may vary from assisting patients to maneuvering heavy equipment. It can lead to strain and fatigue
  3. Diagnostic radiologists are losing out on work due to their high turnover rate. There is currently a shortage of radiologists.
