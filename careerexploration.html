Careers are related to X-Rays

 

 

 

 

Career Options

Operating x-rays machines require a professional because it is a very precise and sensitive machine. These careers fall under different types of radiologist for radiography. They may vary about the purpose of their profession from techincian, engineer, therapist to a specialist.

Radiologic Technologist Runs X-ray machines and other diagnostic imaging procedures to assist doctors in making an appropriate diagnosis and treatment plan for diseases
Radiation Therapist Someone who specializes in treating patients with radiation
Radiologist Specializes in detecting and treating ailments by utilizing imaging technologies.

 

 

Responsibilities and Education

Education requirements vary from each country but in this exploration I am focusing in Saskatchewan, Canada. All of the options above need at least two to three years in their respective fields. I've also chosen these careers to be in clinical and medicine than laboratory.

 

Radiologic Technologist and Radiation Therapist

  • Completion of a two- to three-year college or other approved program in diagnostic radiography or magnetic resonance imaging and a period of supervised practical training are required.
  • Licensure with a regulatory body (Saskatchewan Association of Medical Radiation Technologists) and certification by the Canadian Association of Medical Radiation Technologists are required

 

Radiologist

  • A bachelor's degree or, completion of a college program and one year of pre-medicine university studies are usually required.
  • A medical school degree that has been authorized as well as specialized training are necessary.
  • Four to five years of specialty residency training are required.
  • Completion of the certifying examinations of the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada
